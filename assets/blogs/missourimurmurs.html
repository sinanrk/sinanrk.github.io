Missouri Murmurs

May 11, 2025

A fine Saturday evening. The weather in Omaha is getting gorgeous. Gloomy days of winter are on its way out. Here I am, sitting ashore Missouri, thanking my privilege to breathe this beautiful air, to hear the soothing sound of the big muddy. As I watch her, my thoughts travel along her flow. She has a lots of stories to tell, like my old grandmother. I tried to listen. From her headwaters up in the North, to the moment she joins her beloved Mississippi, until they fall into the lap of Atlantic, she sees myriad lives, joys, and struggles. Happy stories of thriving civilizations on her shoulders, adventures of early explorers, sorrows of exploitations. I tried harder to listen her. But the old lady is weak now, with a stumbling voice. Maybe so, no one hears her. Her veins are getting dryer. "I provided everything to them, but now I am forgotten to be taken care of" she murmured in her weak voice. It sounded like a mother ignored by her kins. Can a hydrologist console her? I tried even harder to listen her.
