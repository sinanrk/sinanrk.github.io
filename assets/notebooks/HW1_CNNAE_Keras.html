SECTION 3: Discussion

Structure-1, an autoencoder with 3 concolutional layers in encoder and decoder, shows a better reconstruction of images visually. This suggests that the encoder of structure-1 can extract the most representative feature of the image, using which the decoders can recontruct images that looks close to inputs. Adding a new convolutional layer (structure-2), reduces the similarity between inputs and outputs, showing a reduced ability to extract most representative features. This suggest a loss of information possibly when passing from 3rd layer to a smaller 4th layer. On the other hand, adding the dense layer (structure-3) works better than that of adding convolutional layer (structure-2), indicating that dense layer is able to retain more representative information of images than that of convolution layer.

Few other things I noticed during the implementation are:

1. It is important to normalize the data before training, as the model may not converge otherwise.

2. With an 8-core cpu, training a Convolutional Autoecoder with 50 epochs of 10000 samples as batches of 128 would take around 40 minutes to complete. A faster training can be achieved with GPU-based training.
